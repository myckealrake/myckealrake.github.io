Recording projects

Over the years I have been learning and applying new ideas and techniques to my recording and mixing practices. From humble beginnings in a basement studio in high school to my current mixes in a modern studio, everything has helped me learn and grow in the field of music production.

Liberty Hall Collective, a group of talented musicians starting off in their dorm hall Made their way through an Iowa battle of the bands to win their grand prize, a recording in Catamount studios in Iowa. While they opted to mix it themselves, they still needed someone for a master of the Catamount Ep as well as their previously released album Lights. I was happy to step to the challenge.

Wanting to up their game and have a more polished touch, the Buena Vista Chapel Band came to myself and a colleague of mine, Brett P, to record a few covers. The first four songs here are our final results from a few weeks that they have put out to share. The last is a bonus live track from them. This was a great multi-week task with many scheduling challenges and creative avenues.

This song was a special recording. A radio competition came up for KBVU 97.5 for a best recorded cover. An unlikely individual came to me asking to record a song dear to her heart. She was a bit inexperienced, but had a beautiful voice that shines next to a Steinway.Both were recorded with an AKG 414, with the piano being recorded Mid/Side with the 414 and a Shure SM81 for the mid.

C U Next Time Was a mixing project for my advanced mixing class. We were provided a Pro Tools file with blank stems and asked to mix the project. This was my final version, mixed and mastered.
